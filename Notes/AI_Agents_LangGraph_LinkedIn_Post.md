Just completed AI Agents in LangGraph by DeepLearning.AI — and this one changed how I think about building production agentic systems.

Most "agent" tutorials show you a loop. This course shows you an architecture.

Here's what the 6-lesson curriculum actually covers:

🔷 Lesson 1 — Build a ReAct agent from scratch (Python + LLM, no frameworks)
🔷 Lesson 2 — Rebuild it in LangGraph: StateGraph, Nodes, Edges, conditional routing
🔷 Lesson 3 — Agentic search with Tavily: structured multi-result answers, not just links
🔷 Lesson 4 — Persistence: state management across threads, time-travel debugging
🔷 Lesson 5 — Human-in-the-Loop: interrupt points, approval flows, state injection
🔷 Lesson 6 — Capstone: Essay Writing Agent (multi-agent Planner → Research → Writer → Critique reflection loop)

The biggest insight: persistence and human-in-the-loop aren't nice-to-haves. They are the difference between a demo and a deployable system.

For anyone building GenAI in enterprise — banking, aviation, retail — the human-in-the-loop pattern is the thing that makes agents safe enough to actually ship.

Taught by Harrison Chase (co-founder, LangChain) and Rotem Weiss (co-founder, Tavily) — both building the actual tools, not just talking about them.
